# Provenance Guard вҖ” Planning & Spec

> Written before implementation. This document is the contract the code implements
> and the prompt source for AI-assisted code generation in M3вҖ“M5.

## Problem & Goal

Creative platforms need to tell readers whether a submitted piece of text was
written by a human or generated by AI вҖ” **not to police creativity, but to
protect attribution and give readers honest context.** Provenance Guard is a
backend service a platform plugs into: it classifies submitted text, scores its
confidence honestly (including "I don't know"), returns a plain-language
transparency label, and lets creators appeal.

**Design north star вҖ” asymmetric error cost.** On a writing platform, falsely
branding a *human's* work as AI is more damaging than missing some AI text. Every
design decision below leans toward *not* crying "AI" unless the evidence is
strong: a high threshold to call AI, an explicit "uncertain" band, and a label
that frames AI verdicts as "signals suggest" rather than accusations.

---

## Architecture Narrative

The path one piece of text takes from submission to the label a reader sees:

1. A creator (via the platform) sends `POST /submit` with `text` + `creator_id`.
2. **Rate limiter** (Flask-Limiter) checks the caller is within limits; over-limit
   requests get `429` before any work is done.
3. The text goes to the **detection pipeline**, which runs two *independent* signals:
   - **Signal 1 вҖ” LLM classifier (Groq):** holistic semantic/stylistic read.
   - **Signal 2 вҖ” Stylometric heuristics:** structural statistics in pure Python.
4. **Confidence scoring** combines the two signal scores into a single
   `ai_likelihood` вҲҲ [0,1], derives the `attribution` band and a `confidence`.
5. **Label generation** maps the band to one of three plain-language label variants.
6. **Storage** persists the content record (status `classified`) and writes a
   structured **audit-log** entry.
7. The endpoint returns `content_id`, `attribution`, `confidence`, `ai_likelihood`,
   per-signal scores, and the `label`.

Appeal flow: a creator sends `POST /appeal` with the `content_id` and their
`creator_reasoning`. The system updates that content's status to `under_review`,
writes an `appeal` entry to the audit log (alongside the original decision), and
returns a confirmation. No automated re-classification вҖ” it routes to a human queue.

## The Five Questions

### 1. Detection signals

Two **genuinely independent** signals вҖ” one semantic, one structural вҖ” so their
combination is more informative than either alone.

**Signal 1 вҖ” LLM classifier (Groq `llama-3.3-70b-versatile`).**
- *Measures:* holistic semantic and stylistic coherence вҖ” does the text *read* as
  human or machine-generated, accounting for meaning, voice, and naturalness?
- *Output:* JSON `{"ai_likelihood": 0.0вҖ“1.0, "reasoning": "..."}`. We force a strict
  JSON contract and clamp to [0,1].
- *Why:* captures things statistics can't вҖ” semantic flatness, generic "helpful
  assistant" voice, suspiciously balanced argumentation.
- *Blind spot:* LLM detectors are unreliable and biased вҖ” they over-flag formal,
  non-native-English, or carefully-edited human prose, and can be fooled by lightly
  paraphrased AI text. It is also non-deterministic. That's exactly why it is *not*
  trusted alone.

**Signal 2 вҖ” Stylometric heuristics (pure Python).**
- *Measures:* structural statistical fingerprints. We compute several sub-metrics
  and fold them into one `stylo_score`:
  - **Burstiness** вҖ” coefficient of variation of sentence lengths. Humans vary
    sentence length a lot (short punch, long meander); AI is more uniform. Low CV вҶ’ more AI-like.
  - **Lexical diversity (MATTR)** вҖ” moving-average type-token ratio, length-robust.
    Extremely uniform/mid-range diversity leans AI; very high or very low leans human.
  - **Contraction & casual-punctuation rate** вҖ” humans use "don't", "won't",
    "?!", "...", ALL CAPS for emphasis; default AI prose rarely does. More casual markers вҶ’ more human.
  - **AI clichГ© phrases** вҖ” "it is important to note", "furthermore", "moreover",
    "delve", "tapestry", "navigate the complexities", "in today's world". Hits вҶ’ more AI.
- *Output:* `stylo_score` вҲҲ [0,1] (AI-likelihood) plus a `breakdown` dict of the raw sub-metrics.
- *Why:* deterministic, explainable, free, and captures *form* the LLM ignores.
- *Blind spot:* it's a proxy for form, not authorship. Short texts have unstable
  statistics; formal human writing (legal, academic) looks "uniform" and over-flags;
  AI told to "write casually" can defeat it. Hence it is *not* trusted alone either.

**Combination.** Weighted average of the two AI-likelihoods:
`ai_likelihood = w_llm * llm_score + w_stylo * stylo_score`, with **w_llm = 0.65,
w_stylo = 0.35**. The LLM gets more weight because it reasons about meaning; the
stylometric signal acts as a structural check that keeps the LLM honest. If the
Groq key is missing or the call fails, the system **degrades gracefully**: it uses
stylometry alone, lowers the reported confidence, and notes the degraded mode in
the response and audit log (never silently pretends the LLM ran).

### 2. Uncertainty representation

- `ai_likelihood` вҲҲ [0,1] = combined probability the text is AI-generated.
- `confidence` = `max(ai_likelihood, 1 вҲ’ ai_likelihood)` вҲҲ [0.5, 1.0] = the
  probability mass behind whichever verdict the system leans toward. **0.5 means a
  coin flip вҖ” "I genuinely don't know."** As evidence concentrates toward either
  pole, confidence rises toward 1.0. This is why 0.51 and 0.95 produce very
  different labels: 0.51 is an honest shrug, 0.95 is a strong call.
- **Bands (asymmetric, conservative about calling AI):**
  | ai_likelihood | attribution    | band meaning              |
  |---------------|----------------|---------------------------|
  | вүҘ 0.70        | `likely_ai`    | strong AI signal          |
  | 0.35 вҖ“ 0.70   | `uncertain`    | not enough evidence       |
  | вүӨ 0.35        | `likely_human` | strong human signal       |
  The bands are deliberately **asymmetric**: the AI threshold (0.70) sits farther
  above the 0.5 midpoint than the human threshold (0.35) sits below it, widening
  the `uncertain` band on the AI side. Borderline text вҖ” especially formal or
  non-native-English human prose вҖ” falls into `uncertain` rather than `likely_ai`,
  protecting human writers from the costliest error (a false positive). This was
  validated against live runs: the formal-economics paragraph scored 0.66 and
  correctly landed in `uncertain` instead of being branded AI.
- **Calibration approach:** sub-metrics are mapped to [0,1] with simple, documented
  piecewise/linear functions tuned against the project's reference inputs (clearly
  AI, clearly human, two borderline). We validate by running those four inputs
  through `scripts/eval.py` and confirming the *ordering* and *band placement* match
  intuition вҖ” we care about meaningful separation, not false precision.

### 3. Transparency label design (three variants вҖ” final text)

Written for a non-technical reader. Each shows a short verdict line, a confidence
phrase (not a raw number alone), and a context sentence. We avoid accusatory
language on AI verdicts.

**High-confidence AI** (`attribution = likely_ai`):
> рҹӨ– **Likely AI-generated** вҖ” Our analysis suggests this text was probably created
> with the help of AI (confidence: {confidence_pct}%). This is an automated signal,
> not a certainty. If you're the creator and disagree, you can appeal this label.

**High-confidence human** (`attribution = likely_human`):
> вңҚпёҸ **Likely human-written** вҖ” Our analysis found no strong signs of AI generation
> in this text (confidence: {confidence_pct}%). No label is perfect, but this reads
> as human-written work.

**Uncertain** (`attribution = uncertain`):
> вқ“ **Authorship unclear** вҖ” Our analysis couldn't confidently determine whether
> this text was written by a human or AI (confidence: {confidence_pct}%). We're
> showing this honestly rather than guessing. Treat the origin as unverified.

### 4. Appeals workflow

- **Who:** the creator of a classified piece (in this backend, anyone who supplies a
  valid `content_id`; a real platform would gate this to the authenticated author).
- **They provide:** `content_id` + `creator_reasoning` (free text, e.g. "I'm a
  non-native speaker and write formally").
- **System does:** validates the `content_id` exists; sets content status
  `classified вҶ’ under_review`; writes an `appeal` audit entry that carries the
  appeal reasoning *and* a snapshot of the original decision (attribution,
  confidence, signal scores); returns a confirmation with the new status. No
  automated re-classification.
- **Human reviewer queue view:** opening the appeal queue (any `content` row with
  status `under_review`, joined to its audit history) shows: the original text, the
  original attribution + confidence + both signal scores, the creator's reasoning,
  and timestamps вҖ” everything a human needs to overturn or uphold the call.

### 5. Anticipated edge cases (where the system is weak)

1. **Formal / non-native-English human prose** (e.g. the monetary-policy paragraph).
   Low burstiness + few contractions + academic register make stylometry score it
   AI-like, and LLM detectors are known to bias against non-native writers. This is
   the exact false-positive we most want to avoid вҖ” the conservative 0.65 AI
   threshold and the prominent appeal path are the mitigations.
2. **Short or highly-repetitive creative text** (a minimalist poem, a chorus with a
   refrain, a haiku). Tiny token counts make TTR/burstiness statistically
   meaningless, and intentional repetition reads as "uniform" вҶ’ false AI signal.
   Mitigation: very short inputs are pushed toward `uncertain` (low confidence)
   rather than a confident verdict.
3. **Lightly-edited AI output** (human tweaks a few sentences of an AI draft).
   Genuinely mixed authorship has no correct binary answer; we want this to land in
   the `uncertain` band rather than force a false-precision call.

## AI Tool Plan

**M3 вҖ” submission endpoint + first signal.**
- *Provide to AI:* the *Detection signals* section (Signal 1) + the Architecture diagram.
- *Ask for:* Flask app skeleton with `POST /submit` route stub + `llm_signal(text)`
  returning `{ai_likelihood, reasoning}`; structured audit log + `GET /log`.
- *Verify:* call `llm_signal` directly on 2вҖ“3 inputs and inspect the JSON; confirm
  the route returns `content_id`/`attribution`/placeholder confidence; confirm each
  submit writes a structured log entry. Edit signatures to match this spec before wiring in.

**M4 вҖ” second signal + confidence scoring.**
- *Provide to AI:* *Detection signals* (Signal 2) + *Uncertainty representation* + diagram.
- *Ask for:* `stylometric_signal(text)` returning `{stylo_score, breakdown}` and
  `combine(llm_score, stylo_score)` returning `{ai_likelihood, confidence, attribution}`.
- *Check:* the combiner uses **exactly** w_llm=0.65/w_stylo=0.35 and the 0.35/0.65
  bands from this doc (AI tools often drift); run the 4 reference inputs and confirm
  clearly-AI vs clearly-human scores separate meaningfully and bands match intuition.

**M5 вҖ” production layer.**
- *Provide to AI:* *Transparency label* variants + *Appeals workflow* + diagram.
- *Ask for:* `make_label(attribution, confidence)` producing the three exact texts,
  and `POST /appeal`.
- *Verify:* generate all three variants and diff against the text in this doc;
  confirm an appeal flips status to `under_review`, logs reasoning + original
  decision, and is visible via `GET /log`; confirm Flask-Limiter returns 429 past the limit.
